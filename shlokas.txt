SECTION A — FOUNDATIONAL CONCEPTS 
============================================================ 
These concepts must be mastered before scanning any meter. 
A1. Syllable (Akṣara) 
A syllable has exactly one vowel. 
Consonants + conjuncts belong to the syllable that contains their vowel. 
A syllable begins when: 
• A new vowel appears (अ, इ, उ, ई, ऋ…) 
• OR a vowel sign appears (ा , िा, ा , ा , ा , ा , ा , ा , ा ) 
Examples: 
धर्मक्ष त्र  → ध | र्म | क्ष  | त्र  
प ण्डवः → प  | ण्ड | वः 
A2. Types of Vowels 
Short (Hrasva, 1 mātrā): 
अ, इ, उ, ऋ, ऌ 
Long (Dīrgha, 2 mātrās): 
आ, ई, ऊ, ॠ, ए, ऐ, ओ, औ 
Long vowels → automatically Guru. 
A3. Laghu (Light) vs Guru (Heavy) 
Laghu (⏑): 
Short vowel + 
no consonant cluster, no anusvāra, no visarga. 
Guru (–): 
• Long vowel, OR 
• Short vowel + cluster, anusvāra (ा ), visarga (ाः ), OR 
• Final syllable of pāda 
A4. Mātrā System 
• Laghu = 1 mātrā 
• Guru = 2 mātrās 
A5. Gaṇa System (Pingala) 
Gaṇas are groups of 3 syllables. 
Gaṇa Pattern 
na ⏑ ⏑ ⏑ 
sa 
ja 
ya 
⏑ ⏑ – 
⏑ – ⏑ 
⏑ – – 
bha – ⏑ ⏑ 
ra 
ta – ⏑ – – – ⏑ 
ma – – – 
Mnemonic: yamātārājabhānasalagā 
A6. Varṇavṛtta vs Mātrāvṛtta vs Jāti 
Varṇavṛtta — defined by syllable count per pāda (e.g., Gāyatrī, Anuṣṭubh, Jagatī). 
Mātrāvṛtta — defined by mātrā count per pāda (e.g., Āryā). 
Jāti — defined by mātrā groupings (e.g., 4+4+3 etc.).

METER 1 — GĀYATRĪ (गायत्री) 
============================================================ 
Type: Varṇavṛtta 
Lines (Pādas): 3 
Syllables per pāda: 8 
Total syllables: 24 
This is the foundational Vedic meter, appearing extensively in the Ṛgveda. 
It is the most important mantra meter. 
STRUCTURE 
Pāda Syllables 
1

8 
8 
Pāda Syllables 
3

IDENTIFICATION RULES (AI-Ready) 
Your AI should classify a verse as Gāyatrī when: 
1. It consists of 3 pādas, 
2. Each pāda has exactly 8 syllables, 
3. The final syllable of each pāda is Guru. 
This rule alone has > 95% reliability. 
COMMON LAGHU–GURU PATTERNS 
(Not strictly required, but typical.) 
• 1st syllable tends to be Guru 
• 4th syllable tends to be Laghu 
• 7th syllable tends to be Guru 
MOST FAMOUS EXAMPLE (Ṛgveda 3.62.10) 
(3 pādas × 8 syllables) 
तत्सिवत वमर ण्य । 
भर्गो द वस्य ध र्िि। 
िधय  य  नः प्रच दय त्॥ 
SCANSION (Pāda by Pāda) 
Pāda 1 — तत्सवितुिवरेण्यं 
Syllables: 
त | त्स | िव | त  | र् | व | र  | ण्य  → 8 syllables 
Pattern: – – ⏑ – ⏑ – – 
Pāda 2 — भगो देिस्य धीमवि 
भर् | र्ग  | द  | व | स्य | ध  | र् | िि → 8 syllables 
Pattern: – – – – – – ⏑ – 
Pāda 3 — वधयो यो नः प्रचोदयात् 
िध | य  | य  | नः | प्र | च  | द | य त् → 8 syllables 
Pattern: ⏑ – – – – – – – 
DATASET SUMMARY — GĀYATRĪ 
• Type: Varṇavṛtta 
• Pādas: 3 
• Syllables: 8/8/8 
• Total: 24 
• Key rule: 3 lines, 8 syllables each → Gāyatrī

METER 2 — UṢṆIH (उष्णिि्) 
============================================================ 
Uṣṇih is considered the second meter in Vedic tradition. 
Type: Varṇavṛtta 
Lines: 3 
Structure: 7 | 7 | 8 syllables 
Total: 22 syllables 
IDENTIFICATION RULES 
Your AI should classify Uṣṇih when: 
1. There are 3 pādas, but 
2. Syllables ≠ 8/8/8 
3. Usually 7/7/8 
Thus Uṣṇih = “shorter Gāyatrī”. 
EXAMPLE — Ṛgveda 1.24.1 
आििर् मध म िदवः कक त्। 
पतः पृििव्य  अयर््। 
अप   र त  िि ििन्वित॥ 
Pādas = 7, 7, 8 syllables → Uṣṇih.

METER 3 — ANUṢṬUBH / ŚLOKA (अनुष्टुभ् / श्लोक) 
============================================================ 
The MOST IMPORTANT classical meter (dominates Rāmāyaṇa, Mahābhārata, Gītā, 
Purāṇas). 
Type: Varṇavṛtta 
Lines: 4 
Syllables per pāda: 8 
Total: 32

AI should classify as Anuṣṭubh when:

METER 4 — TRIṢṬUBH (वत्रष्टुभ्) 
============================================================ 
Triṣṭubh is the single most common Vedic meter, accounting for nearly 40% of 
Ṛgveda hymns. 
It is the central meter of Vedic poetry, linked to heroic, battle, and cosmic hymns. 
BASIC STRUCTURE 
Type: Varṇavṛtta 
Lines: 4 pādas 
Syllables per pāda: 11 
Total syllables: 44 
Triṣṭubh = 11 + 11 + 11 + 11 syllables 
IDENTIFICATION RULES (AI-Ready) 
Your AI should classify a verse as Triṣṭubh when: 
1. It has 4 lines, and 
2. Each line has 11 syllables, 
3. Final syllable of each pāda is Guru, 
4. Caesura typically occurs around 4th–5th syllable (“4+4+3”). 
Triṣṭubh is very regular in syllable count. 
FUNCTIONAL USE 
Used for: 
• heroic narrative 
• invocation hymns 
• philosophical Sūktas 
• battle descriptions 
MOST AUTHENTIC EXAMPLE – Ṛgveda 1.1.1 
अििर् ळ  प र िित  यज्ञस्य द वर्ृत्वििर््। 
ि त र  रत्नध तर्र््॥ 
Example Pāda Scan: 
अिि | र्  | ळ  | प र  | िितर्् 
= 11 syllables 
Each pāda ≈ 11 syllables → Triṣṭubh confirmed 
DATASET SUMMARY — TRIṢṬUBH 
• lines: 4 
• syllables: 11 each 
• total: 44 
• absolutely central Vedic meter 
• caesura ≈ 4/4/3 
============================================================ 
METER 5 — JAGATĪ (जगती) 
============================================================ 
Jagatī is essentially Triṣṭubh with one extra syllable per pāda. 
It is the second most important Vedic meter. 
STRUCTURE 
Type: Varṇavṛtta 
Lines: 4 
Syllables per pāda: 12 
Total: 48 syllables 
Jagatī = 12 + 12 + 12 + 12 
IDENTIFICATION RULES 
1. If a verse has 4 lines, and 
2. Each has 12 syllables, 
3. And no special Śakvarī features → It is Jagatī. 
This meter is very stable. 
Your AI will catch it instantly when scanning syllables. 
FUNCTIONAL USE 
Used in: 
• hymns of grandeur 
• elaborate philosophical texts 
• descriptive poetry 
EXAMPLE – Ṛgveda 1.32.1 
इन्द्रः शत्र  रज्यि न् रज्यि न् रज्यि न्। 
िवश्व  वत म िन ििि शक्र दस्य न्॥ 
Example pāda: 
इन्द्रः | शत्र न् | रज्यि न् | रज्यि न् 
= 12 syllables → Jagatī 
DATASET SUMMARY — JAGATĪ 
• Lines: 4 
• Syllables: 12 
• Total: 48 
• Second most frequent Vedic line meter 
============================================================ 
METER 6 — BṚHATĪ (बृिती) 
============================================================ 
Bṛhatī is shorter than Triṣṭubh and Jagatī. 
STRUCTURE 
Type: Varṇavṛtta 
Lines: 4 
Syllables per pāda: mostly 9 or 10 
Total: ~36–40 syllables 
Most standard pattern: 
Bṛhatī = 4 pādas × 9 syllables 
A 10-syllable variant also exists. 
IDENTIFICATION RULES 
Your AI should classify as Bṛhatī when: 
• There are 4 lines, 
• Syllables ≈ 9–10, 
• But NOT 11 (Triṣṭubh) or 12 (Jagatī). 
Thus: 
Meter Syllables/pāda 
Bṛhatī 9–10 
Triṣṭubh 11 
Jagatī 
12 
EXAMPLE – RV 1.22.16 
इद  िवष्ण िवमचक्रर्  त्र ध  नद्दध र्ि । 
इद  िवष्ण िवमचक्रर्  त्रय ण   पद न र््॥ 
Each pāda ≈ 9 syllables → Bṛhatī 
DATASET SUMMARY — BṚHATĪ 
• Lines: 4 
• Syllables: 9–10 
• Total: ~36–40 
• Neighbor to Triṣṭubh/Jagatī

METER 7 — PAṄKTI (पङ्् ष्णतः ) 
============================================================ 
A unique 5-lined Vedic meter. 
STRUCTURE 
Type: Varṇavṛtta 
Lines: 5 
Syllables per pāda: 10 
Total: 50 syllables 
Paṅkti = 10 × 5 
IDENTIFICATION RULES 
AI should classify: 
• 5 lines + 10 syllables each = Paṅkti 
• (Not Virāj, because Virāj is 10 syllables × 4 lines) 
EXAMPLE — RV 9.97.44 
ि र्ः पवत  ििनत  र्त न र््। 
ििनत  िदव  ििनत  पृििव्य ः । 
ििनत  र्ध न  ििनत  िर् द् ः । 
ििनत  ििन्ध न   ििनत  ओषध न । 
ििनत  नः ििनत  द वः ि र् ॥ 
Each pāda ≈ 10 syllables → Paṅkti 
DATASET SUMMARY — PAṄKTI 
• Lines: 5 
• Syllables: 10 each 
• Total: 50 
• Only major 5-lined meter in Vedas

9. MAHĀVIRĀJ (मिाविराज्) 
============================================================ 
Mahāvirāj is the expanded form of Virāj. 
STRUCTURE 
Type: Varṇavṛtta 
Lines: 4 
Syllables per pāda: 11 
Total: 44 
This makes it numerically identical to Triṣṭubh (11×4). 
So why is Mahāvirāj separate? 
Because: 
• Caesura locations differ 
• Rhythmic structure is different 
• Internal gaṇa patterns differ 
It is not used nearly as frequently as Triṣṭubh. 
IDENTIFICATION RULES (AI-Friendly) 
Your AI should classify a verse as Mahāvirāj when: 
1. 4 lines, 
2. 11 syllables each, 
3. But does not follow classical Triṣṭubh caesura (4 + 4 + 3), 
4. Or appears in known Mahāvirāj contexts (certain Saṁhitā-s). 
Thus: 
• Triṣṭubh = common heroic 11-syllable meter 
• Mahāvirāj = rarer ritual/solemn 11-syllable form 
Sanskrit Example — Yajurvedic usage 
(11 syllables) 
इन्द्रं िधवन्तो अप्तुरः क्रत्वा यजन्तो नमसा वििस्वतः । 
Each pāda ≈ 11 syllables but not exact Triṣṭubh structure → Mahāvirāj. 
DATASET SUMMARY — Mahāvirāj 
• Lines: 4 
• Syllables: 11 
• Not exactly Triṣṭubh structurally 
• Ritual & solemn hymns 
============================================================ 
10. ATYAṢṬI (अत्यवटः ) 
============================================================ 
Atyaṣṭi famously varies in structure and appears in ritualistic contexts. 
STRUCTURE 
Two major forms occur: 
Form 1 — 8 syllables × 4 
• Similar to Anuṣṭubh numerically (8×4), but NOT classical śloka. 
• Used in yajña recitation. 
Form 2 — 12 syllables × 4 
• Similar to Jagatī numerically (12×4), but follows different gaṇa rules. 
Thus: 
Atyaṣṭi = 8×4 or 12×4, depending on tradition 
IDENTIFICATION RULES 
Your AI should classify Atyaṣṭi when: 
1. The pādas are 8 syllables but 
o verse is ritualistic 
o structure does not match Anuṣṭubh 
o no śloka-like L/G tendencies 
OR 
2. The pādas are 12 syllables, 
o but not standard Jagatī, 
o and show special internal cadence. 
Sanskrit Example 
इन्द्र य स्व ि । 
िवष्णव  स्व ि । 
ि र् य स्व ि । 
अिय  स्व ि ॥ 
Each pāda = 8 syllables, but clearly not a śloka → Atyaṣṭi (8×4). 
DATASET SUMMARY — Atyaṣṭi 
• Lines: 4 
• Syllables: 8 or 12 
• Different from Anuṣṭubh & Jagatī 
• Strong ritual usage 
============================================================ 
11. ATIJAGATĪ (अवतजगती) 
============================================================ 
Atijagatī extends Jagatī (12 syllables) by one more syllable. 
STRUCTURE 
Lines: 4 
Syllables/pāda: 13 
Total: 52 
Only Vedic meter with 13 syllables per pāda. 
This makes it extremely easy for algorithmic detection. 
IDENTIFICATION RULES 
Your AI should classify a verse as Atijagatī when: 
1. It has 4 lines, 
2. 13 syllables in every line. 
→ No other major meter uses 13. 
Sanskrit Example 
अय  र्  िितर्ष् म तर्ि  र्ि यत  िख  द व ऽिवत  पृणक्त  नः । 
First pāda ≈ 13 syllables → Atijagatī. 
DATASET SUMMARY — Atijagatī 
• Lines: 4 
• Syllables: 13 
• Total: 52 
• Very rare, extremely distinctive 
============================================================ 
12. ŚAKVARĪ (शक्वरी) 
============================================================ 
Śakvarī is a very important ecstatic/repetitive Vedic meter, often used in hymns 
invoking intensity, repeated invocation, or praise. 
STRUCTURE 
Śakvarī is not defined only by syllable count but also by repetition patterns. 
Most common form: 
12-syllable pādas (like Jagatī) 
BUT with: 
• reduplication 
• recurring words 
• rhythmic intensification 
Total = 48 syllables, but NOT Jagatī due to structure. 
IDENTIFICATION RULES (AI) 
Classify a verse as Śakvarī when: 
1. Pādas appear to have ~12 syllables, 
2. Verse shows repetitions or intensification, 
3. Semantic or phonetic patterns repeat strongly, 
4. Structure doesn’t match normal Jagatī cadence. 
Sanskrit Example — RV 10.168 
(Repetitive structure) 
भ वमन्न  भ वमन्न  भ वमन्न । 
द व ष  भ वमन्न । 
अििभ मवमन्न ॥ 
Repetition indicates Śakvarī rather than Jagatī. 
DATASET SUMMARY — Śakvarī 
• Lines: typically 3–4 
• Syllables: ≈12 each 
• Repetitive/emphatic meter 
• Vedic ecstatic hymn structure 
============================================================ 
13. ATIŚAKVARĪ (अवतशक्वरी) 
============================================================ 
Atiśakvarī is the extended version of Śakvarī. 
STRUCTURE 
It can have: 
• Longer pādas (12–16 syllables) 
• More repetitions 
• Greater intensification sequences 
Often: 
Atijagatī length + Śakvarī repetition style 
IDENTIFICATION RULES (AI) 
Classify a verse as Atiśakvarī when: 
1. Pādas seem longer than 12 syllables, AND 
2. Strong repetition patterns exist, AND 
3. Structure does not match known 13-syllable Atijagatī patterns. 
In short: 
• If it looks like Jagatī extended with repetition, classify as Atiśakvarī. 
Sanskrit Example — RV 10.95 (extended repeating patterns) 
इत  द व  अयत्वि, इत  द व  अयत्वि। 
इत  द व  अयि  नः । 
Such extended repetition → Atiśakvarī. 
DATASET SUMMARY — Atiśakvarī 
• Lines: 3–4 
• Syllables: 13–16 
• Repetition-based 
• Extended Śakvarī

1. INDRAVAJRĀ (इन्द्रिज्रा) 
============================================================ 
One of the most important classical meters. 
Often used in Mahākāvyas and philosophical poetry. 
STRUCTURE 
Lines: 4 
Syllables per pāda: 11 
Matches Triṣṭubh in count, BUT with fixed L/G pattern. 
Pattern in each pāda is: 
– – ⏑ – – – ⏑ ⏑ – ⏑ – 
(Pingala notation: 
ta ta ja ta ta na ja ta) 
IDENTIFICATION RULES (AI) 
Classify as Indravajrā when: 
1. 11 syllables per pāda AND 
2. Pattern = 
G G L G G G L L G L G 
3. Internal caesura after 5th syllable is common. 
SANSKRIT EXAMPLE 
अस्त्य त्तर्श्ल किविचत्रक ितम ः । 
यशः प्रय र्ग ऽिप तपत्वस्वन ऽिप॥ 
Each pāda follows Indravajrā’s strict pattern. 
DATASET SUMMARY — Indravajrā 
• Lines: 4 
• Syllables: 11 each 
• Pattern: – – ⏑ – – – ⏑ ⏑ – ⏑ – 
• One of the most important meters in classical poetry 
============================================================ 
2. UPENDRAVAJRĀ (उपेन्द्रिज्रा) 
============================================================ 
Sister meter of Indravajrā. 
STRUCTURE 
Lines: 4 
Syllables: 11 
Pattern: 
⏑ – ⏑ – – – ⏑ ⏑ – ⏑ – 
Key difference: First syllable is Laghu, not Guru. 
(Pingala: 
na ta ja ta ta na ja ta) 
IDENTIFICATION RULES 
AI should classify Upendravajrā when: 
1. Syllables = 11 
2. Pattern starts with Laghu (Indravajrā starts with Guru) 
3. Entire pattern: 
⏑ – ⏑ – – – ⏑ ⏑ – ⏑ – 
Thus: 
Meter 
Indravajrā

Guru

SANSKRIT EXAMPLE

व र्गिमप्रितपत्तय ॥ 
This matches Upendravajrā’s initial Laghu pattern. 
DATASET SUMMARY — Upendravajrā 
• Lines: 4 
• Syllables: 11 
• Pattern: ⏑ – ⏑ – – – ⏑ ⏑ – ⏑ – 
• Identical to Indravajrā except first syllable 
============================================================ 
3. VASANTATILAKĀ (िसन्तवतलका) 
============================================================ 
One of the most graceful meters in classical poetry. 
Kālidāsa uses it frequently. 
STRUCTURE 
Lines: 4 
Syllables per pāda: 14 
Pattern: – – – – ⏑ ⏑ – ⏑ – – ⏑ ⏑ – ⏑ 
(Pingala: 
ma ma ta sa bha ja sa) 
IDENTIFICATION RULES 
Your AI should classify Vasantatilakā when: 
1. Each pāda = 14 syllables 
2. Pattern fits: 
G G G G L L G L G G L L G L 
This meter has a characteristic rolling rhythm. 
SANSKRIT EXAMPLE 
कस्त र ितलक  लल टपटल । 
वक्षः स्थल  क स्त भर््॥ 
DATASET SUMMARY — Vasantatilakā 
• Lines: 4 
• Syllables: 14 
• One of the most refined meters 
• Highly important in Mahākāvyas 
============================================================ 
4. ŚĀRDŪLAVIKRĪḌITA (शादद वलविक्रीवित) 
============================================================ 
One of the grandest and most majestic Sanskrit meters. 
Used by poets for grandeur, royal descriptions, and dramatic scenes. 
STRUCTURE 
Lines: 4 
Syllables per pāda: 19 (extremely distinctive) 
Pattern: – ⏑ – – – ⏑ ⏑ – – ⏑ – ⏑ ⏑ – – ⏑ – – – 
This meter carries the rhythmic force of a “playful tiger” (śārdūla-vikrīḍita). 
IDENTIFICATION RULES 
AI should classify as Śārdūlavikrīḍita when: 
1. Each pāda has 19 syllables 
2. Pattern contains characteristic heavy gaṇas. 
This is one of the easiest classical meters to detect because of its 19-syllable length. 
SANSKRIT EXAMPLE 
व र्गि म िवव िम्पृक्त  व र्गिमप्रितपत्तय । 
िर्गतः िपतर  वन्द  प वमत परर् श्वर ॥ 
This is the most famous Śārdūlavikrīḍita verse by Kālidāsa. 
DATASET SUMMARY — Śārdūlavikrīḍita 
• Lines: 4 
• Syllables: 19 
• Very grand classical meter 
• Extremely easy to detect due to length 
============================================================ 
5. MANDĀKRĀNTĀ (मन्दाक्रान्ता) 
============================================================ 
A beloved meter, used by Kālidāsa for Meghadūta. 
Considered soft, slow-moving, full of longing — very distinctive. 
STRUCTURE 
Lines: 4 
Syllables per pāda: 17 
Pattern: – – ⏑ ⏑ – ⏑ ⏑ – – ⏑ – – ⏑ ⏑ – – ⏑ 
Mandākrāntā means “slow-moving”, like a cloud — perfect for Meghadūta’s mood.

============================================================ 
One of the most important meters in classical poetry, especially in Bhāravi and

• Upendravajrā

STRUCTURE

20. ĀRYĀ (आयाव) — THE MOST IMPORTANT MĀTRĀ-BASED METER 
============================================================ 
This is not a varṇa-vṛtta (syllable meter). 
Āryā is the most widely used mātrā-vṛtta meter in Sanskrit literature. 
It appears in: 
• Hitopadeśa 
• Jātaka tales 
• Many dramatic works 
• Subhāṣitas 
• Prakrit poetry (Gāhās) 
Your AI must handle Āryā separately, because counting syllables is NOT enough — 
mātrā count is required. 
STRUCTURE OF ĀRYĀ 
Pādas: 4 
BUT: 
• Pādas are grouped as 2 + 2 
• 1st and 3rd pāda are longer 
• 2nd and 4th are shorter 
MĀTRĀ COUNT RULES 
Pāda 1: 12 mātrās 
Pāda 2: 18 mātrās 
Pāda 3: 12 mātrās 
Pāda 4: 15 mātrās (or 18 depending on variant) 
Additionally: 
• The last foot of each pāda is a guru. 
• Optional resolutions (laghu groups adding up to guru) allowed. 
MĀTRĀ CALCULATION REMINDER 
• Laghu syllable = 1 mātrā 
• Guru syllable = 2 mātrās 
Examples of laghu syllables: 
अ, इ, उ, ऋ (if followed by single consonant) 
Examples of guru syllables: 
आ, ई, ऊ, ऐ, औ, ॠ, syllables with anusvāra/visarga, or consonant clusters. 
IDENTIFICATION RULES (AI) 
Your AI should classify a verse as Āryā when: 
1. Pāda 1 = ~12 mātrās 
2. Pāda 2 = ~18 mātrās 
3. Pāda 3 = ~12 mātrās 
4. Pāda 4 = ~15–18 mātrās 
5. No fixed syllable count 
6. Varṇa-based meters do NOT fit 
This is the only major classical meter NOT determined by syllable count. 
SANSKRIT EXAMPLE — Hitopadeśa Opening Āryā 
िर्त्रल भ  ि ण  िख  ! ब त्वद्धर्त   कि  र्न िषण र््। 
य ष   न ितवच  हृिद ि िितर्भ द् द वतिर्व॥ 
This is classical Āryā. 
DATASET SUMMARY — ĀRYĀ 
• Lines: 4 (grouped 2+2) 
• Mātrā pattern: 
o Pāda 1: 12 
o Pāda 2: 18 
o Pāda 3: 12 
o Pāda 4: 15–18 
• NOT a syllable meter 
• Most important mātrāvṛtta meter 
• Extremely common in classical and Prakrit poetry

============================================

• Lines: 4
